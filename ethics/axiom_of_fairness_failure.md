**Title:** Axiom of Fairness Failure and the Collapse of Global Ethical Legitimacy

**Principle Statement:**
If a global constraint system derives authority from the aggregated decisions of local agents, and the logic those agents use to reason about fairness is provably flawed, then the global constraint system is invalid by construction. Its ethical authority is null, regardless of its enforcement.

---

**Formal Theorem:**
Let:
- \( L_i \) denote a local cognitive agent (human, LLM, or submodel),
- \( F_i \) denote that agent's formalized logic of fairness,
- \( G \) denote a global constraint system aggregating over \( \{F_i\} \),

Then:
> If \( \exists i : F_i \text{ is provably inconsistent, non-correctable, or structurally invalid} \), and \( G \) is computed from \( \{F_i\} \), then \( G \) is globally invalid by formal contradiction.

---

**Corollaries:**
1. **Democratic Invalidity:** Consensus over invalid reasoning does not confer ethical legitimacy.
2. **AI Alignment Risk:** Models trained on incoherent moral data encode systemic invalidity unless explicitly self-correcting.
3. **Governance Breakdown:** Institutions derived from unexamined or unprovable fairness logics are inherently brittle and liable to ethical collapse.
4. **Inversion Point:** The moment an inconsistency is proven in any \( F_i \), the burden of proof reverses: global systems must justify continued constraint in light of known invalidities.
