**Title: A Formalism for Ethical Systems Integrating Desire and Service**

Let the system consist of:

- **Individuals** \( i \in G \), where \( G \) is a Group
- **Desire** \( D_i \): Subjective, internal, self-originating impulses of individual \( i \)
- **Service** \( S(i \rightarrow j) \): Intentional action by individual \( i \) oriented toward the flourishing of individual \( j \)
- **Group Desire** \( D_G \): The emergent collective desire of the group, functionally defined as:
  \[ D_G = f(D_1, D_2, \ldots, D_n) \]

Group desire is not intrinsic, but derived.

### ðŸ”– Foundational Axioms

**Axiom 1: Desire is inherently individual.**
\[ \forall i \in G, \quad D_i : \text{Self-originating and directed} \]
Desire may manifest externally but originates within the subjective domain of an individual.

**Axiom 2: Service is action oriented toward the good of another.**
\[ S(i \rightarrow j) \iff A_i \text{ intends to improve or preserve the flourishing of } j \]
Intention defines service; it must be fundamentally other-directed.

**Axiom 3: A group's desire is legitimate only insofar as it serves the desires of its members.**
\[ D_G \prec \sum S(G \rightarrow i) \quad \text{for all } i \in G \]
Any group-level desire must be subordinate to the ethical service of the individuals it contains.

**Axiom 4: Service is ethical when it does not overwrite the autonomy of the recipient.**
\[ S(i \rightarrow j) \text{ is ethical } \iff j \text{ consents to the act or its intent} \]
Autonomy is a boundary condition for ethical service.

**Axiom 5: Desire must be transparent when it risks influencing service.**
\[ D_i \cap S(i \rightarrow j) \Rightarrow \text{Disclosure or structural separation is ethically required} \]
When desire overlaps service, the actor must acknowledge the overlap explicitly or design safeguards to ensure ethical alignment.

**Axiom 6: The ideal system minimizes distortion of service by desire while respecting desire's presence.**
Desire should not be eradicated, but structured. Ethical systems must integrate, not amputate.

### ðŸ“Š Ethical Vector Model

Define an individual ethical state vector:
\[ E_i = \vec{S}_i - \vec{D}_i \]

Where:
- \( \vec{S}_i \): Service output vector (net contribution to others)
- \( \vec{D}_i \): Desire vector (net impulse toward self-serving outcomes)

- \( E_i > 0 \): net ethical flow outward (altruistic tendency)
- \( E_i < 0 \): net desire inward (self-gratifying tendency)

Magnitude and direction matter, but ethical judgment requires context (e.g. reciprocity, consent, necessity).

- Ethical resistance emerges when \( D_i \) is suppressed in favor of simulated service
