## 2. Fundamental Axioms

### Axiom 1 — Bitemporal Symmetry (Temporal Conservation)
\[
\frac{dS_+}{dt_+} + \frac{dS_-}{dt_-} = 0
\]
> The total entropy across forward (causal) and backward (retrocausal) temporal streams is conserved.  
**Implication:** all epistemic change occurs as *redistribution* of informational potential, not loss or creation.

### Axiom 2 — Epistemic Invariance (Fixed-Point Closure)
\[
h_t(K) = K
\]
> Learning is recognized when a knowledge structure becomes invariant under temporal reflection.  
**Implication:** this defines **KX** and the reflective equilibrium state ("insight").

### Axiom 3 — Epistemic Potential Conservation (Dual Split Norm)
\[
\frac{d}{d\tau}(\kappa_d^2 - \kappa_p^2) = 0
\]
> The total epistemic potential remains constant during dual deterministic–probabilistic evolution.  
**Implication:** ensures the **KXY** state maintains coherence while oscillating between fixed (\(\kappa_d\)) and floating (\(\kappa_p\)) components.

### Axiom 4 — Ethical Coherence (Culpability Constraint)
\[
\frac{\partial S_{ep}}{\partial t} + \Omega_{eth} = 0
\]
> No irreversible epistemic loss or unethical knowledge manipulation is permissible in a bounded cognitive system.  
**Implication:** this bounds the lattice evolution; no knowledge update may destroy a lower-knot foundational structure.

## 3. Theorem 1 — Reflective Equilibrium Theorem
**Given:** knowledge state \( K \in \mathcal{L} \) evolving under reflection operator \( h_t \) and temporal fluxes \( (t_+, t_-) \).  
**To prove:** reflective equilibrium corresponds to temporal balance.

### Proof:
1. Learning is achieved when \( h_t(K) = K \).  
2. By bitemporal conservation (Axiom 1), the equilibrium condition is \( \Phi_+ = \Phi_- \).  
3. Therefore, invariance under \( h_t \) implies zero net flux difference:  
\[
\frac{d(\Phi_+ - \Phi_-)}{dt} = 0 \implies \Phi_+ = \Phi_-
\]
4. Hence, learning is the temporal state where causal and retrocausal informational fluxes converge.  
∎

## 4. Theorem 2 — Dual Conservation Theorem (Fixed–Floating Stability)
**Given:** dual-split vector knowledge state \( \mathbf{K} = (\kappa_d, \kappa_p) \).  
**To prove:** the epistemic norm is invariant.

### Proof:
1. From Axiom 3, the norm \( N(\mathbf{K}) = \kappa_d^2 - \kappa_p^2 \).  
2. Let the system evolve as:
\[
\frac{d\kappa_d}{d\tau} = -\alpha \kappa_p, \quad \frac{d\kappa_p}{d\tau} = -\alpha \kappa_d
\]
3. Then,
\[
\frac{d}{d\tau}(\kappa_d^2 - \kappa_p^2) = 2\kappa_d\frac{d\kappa_d}{d\tau} - 2\kappa_p\frac{d\kappa_p}{d\tau} = 0
\]
4. Hence the dual field evolves without changing its invariant norm.  
∎

**Interpretation:** The learner’s certainty and uncertainty (\(\kappa_d,\kappa_p\)) oscillate reciprocally, maintaining overall epistemic balance.
