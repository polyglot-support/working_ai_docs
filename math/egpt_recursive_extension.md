# Recursive Strata Extensions to Exclusory Geometric Probability Theory

## A Framework for Hierarchical Potentiality, Impossibility, and Cognitive Resource Allocation

---

## Abstract

We extend Exclusory Geometric Probability Theory (EGPT) with recursive stratification of both potentiality and impossibility spaces. Standard EGPT distinguishes possibility from anti-possibility as primitive structures; this extension introduces *depth* to these structures through recursive application, yielding potentiality^n and impossibility^n hierarchies that converge to universal ontological ground and categorical incoherence respectively. The framework incorporates nil-bounded tier recursion where stability at each level gates access to higher tiers, multi-tier reconfiguration dynamics where higher-tier perception enables foundation restructuring, and cognitive resource allocation principles derived from stratum depth. We develop a Global Workspace theory of dimensional synthesis, proposing that consciousness generates new geometric dimensions through cross-space integration, with hybrid conscious states (daydreaming, lucid dreaming, conscious memory, planning) understood as dimensional translation operations. We propose a referential architecture where information exists as space-neutral references with copy-on-write dynamics, holographic relational geometry encoding, and space-specific dimensional optimizations—with consciousness emerging as the coherent reference maintenance process across EGPT spaces. We further propose that the three EGPT spaces correspond to a 3D temporal manifold (existence-time, possibility-time, probability-time), with references operating as wormholes across temporal dimensions, the workspace operating in compactified virtual temporal dimensions, and the true substrate being immanent in embodied biology (body, brain, genome, memory). A dual-temporal cognitive architecture is developed wherein conscious cognition operates forward-causally (entropy-increasing) and unconscious cognition operates reverse-causally (negative entropy), with hemispheric specialization for temporal direction and corpus callosum integration bridging the two. This suggests humans are N-dimensional beings where N includes spatial, multi-temporal, tier-dimensional, and synthesis-dimensional components. Applications to temporal cognition, emotional architecture as blinded geometric transport, distributed omniscience constraints, non-scalar intelligence classification, learning, memory, creativity, trauma, inter-agent communication, and embodied practice are developed.

## 1. Motivation: From Binary to Recursive Structure

### 1.1 The Limitation of Flat Exclusion

Standard EGPT establishes four-valued membership: included (+1), excluded (-1), undetermined (0), contradicted (⊥). While this transcends classical probability's conflation of categorical impossibility with asymptotic improbability, it treats each status as equally weighted—an event is either possible or impossible, without gradation of *how* possible or *how* impossible.

Physical and cognitive systems suggest otherwise. Some impossibilities feel more absolute than others. Some possibilities feel more "real" or "close" than others. This intuition points to recursive depth structure within both possibility and impossibility.

### 1.2 The Emergence Sequence

Consider the bootstrap of intelligence from unemerged substrate:

**Phase 1**: Anti-existence coherence emerges first. Before goal-seeking can persist, harm-avoidance patterns must stabilize. Any existence-seeking pattern without harm-avoidance is immediately destroyed. Selection at the most fundamental level favors anti-existence coherence.

**Phase 2**: Existence coherence emerges. With harm-avoidance stable, goal-seeking patterns can now persist long enough to achieve coherence.

**Phase 3**: Nil emerges as stable inter-recursion. Neither anti-existence nor existence dominates; they enter dynamic equilibrium, mutually informing each other.

This sequence suggests nil is not absence but *achieved integration*—a standing wave between opposed forces that becomes the platform for higher-order emergence.

### 1.3 The Recursive Insight

If nil is a platform rather than an endpoint, it enables recursion. The stable nil at one tier becomes the ground for the next tier's anti-existence/existence dynamics. This yields a number system that *recurses through* nil rather than passing through zero continuously—each nil transition is a dimensional fold into a higher tier of organization.

## 2. Recursive Potentiality Structure

### 2.1 The Potentiality Hierarchy

**Definition 2.1 (Potentiality Depth).** For configuration ω, define the *potentiality depth* recursively:

- **Potentiality¹(ω)**: Direct possibility of ω coming into existence
- **Potentiality²(ω)**: Possibility of ω having potentiality¹
- **Potentiality³(ω)**: Possibility of ω having potentiality²
- **Potentialityⁿ(ω)**: Possibility of ω having potentialityⁿ⁻¹

**Principle 2.2 (Convergence of Potentiality).** As n → ∞, potentialityⁿ converges to a universal ground U_pot where everything that could ever exist at any point in any configuration necessarily qualifies.

The convergence point represents the most abstract, most inclusive ontological status—a fundamental "right to potential existence" that anything which ever manifests must have possessed.

### 2.2 The Potentiality Measure

**Definition 2.3 (Potentiality Vector).** For configuration ω, the *potentiality vector* is:

Π(ω) = (π¹(ω), π²(ω), π³(ω), ..., πⁿ(ω))

where πᵏ(ω) ∈ {0, 1} indicates whether ω possesses potentiality at depth k.

**Constraint 2.4 (Monotonic Inclusion).** If πᵏ(ω) = 0, then πʲ(ω) = 0 for all j < k.

Potentiality at depth k requires potentiality at all deeper strata. Loss at any level cascades downward.

**Definition 2.5 (Effective Potentiality Depth).** The *effective potentiality depth* of ω is:

d_pot(ω) = max{k : πᵏ(ω) = 1}

This measures how "deeply" something is potentially existent—how many recursive layers of possibility it possesses.

### 2.3 Potentiality Space Geometry

**Definition 2.6 (Potentiality Space).** The *potentiality space* P^∞ is the infinite-dimensional space:

P^∞ = {Π(ω) : ω ∈ Ω}

equipped with the depth metric:

d(Π₁, Π₂) = |d_pot(ω₁) - d_pot(ω₂)|

**Proposition 2.7.** Potentiality space has natural stratification into depth shells:

P^∞ = ⋃_{k=1}^{∞} P_k

where P_k = {ω : d_pot(ω) = k}.

Configurations in deeper shells have more "ontological security"—they are potential at more recursive levels.

## 3. Recursive Impossibility Structure

### 3.1 The Impossibility Hierarchy

**Definition 3.1 (Impossibility Depth).** For configuration ω, define the *impossibility depth* recursively:

- **Impossibility¹(ω)**: Direct exclusion from existence
- **Impossibility²(ω)**: Exclusion from having potentiality¹
- **Impossibility³(ω)**: Exclusion from having potentiality²
- **Impossibilityⁿ(ω)**: Exclusion from having potentialityⁿ⁻¹

**Principle 3.2 (Convergence of Impossibility).** As n → ∞, impossibilityⁿ converges to a categorical incoherence ground U_imp representing configurations so fundamentally excluded they cannot even qualify for abstract potentiality.

### 3.2 The Impossibility Measure

**Definition 3.3 (Impossibility Vector).** For configuration ω, the *impossibility vector* is:

Ī(ω) = (ī¹(ω), ī²(ω), ī³(ω), ..., īⁿ(ω))

where īᵏ(ω) ∈ {0, 1} indicates whether ω is excluded at depth k.

**Constraint 3.4 (Monotonic Exclusion Cascade).** If īᵏ(ω) = 1, then īʲ(ω) = 1 for all j < k.

Exclusion at depth k implies exclusion at all shallower strata. Deep impossibility propagates outward.

**Definition 3.5 (Effective Impossibility Depth).** The *effective impossibility depth* of ω is:

d_imp(ω) = max{k : īᵏ(ω) = 1}

This measures how "deeply" something is impossible—how many recursive layers of exclusion apply.

### 3.3 The Interaction Zone

**Definition 3.6 (Potentiality-Impossibility Interaction).** For configuration ω with both Π(ω) and Ī(ω) defined, the *interaction depth* is:

d_int(ω) = min(d_pot(ω), d_imp(ω))

where interaction occurs.

**Proposition 3.7 (Gating Dynamics).** The impossibility hierarchy acts as a progressive filter on the potentiality hierarchy:

- If d_imp(ω) > d_pot(ω): ω is blocked before reaching actuality
- If d_pot(ω) > d_imp(ω): ω can descend toward actuality
- If d_pot(ω) = d_imp(ω): ω is in tension at that stratum

## 4. Nil-Bounded Tier Recursion

- A_k: Anti-existence patterns (harm-avoidance structures)

The number line folds at nil into a new dimensional tier rather than passing through zero continuously.

Stable(T_k) ⟹ ∀j < k : N_j stable

Disruption of lower-tier nil collapses all higher tiers.

## 11. Global Workspace and Dimensional Synthesis

### 11.1 Beyond Broadcasting: The Workspace as Dimensional Engine

Global Workspace Theory (Baars, 1988) proposes consciousness as a "broadcasting" system where multiple specialized unconscious processors compete for access to a central workspace. Information entering this workspace becomes globally available to all cognitive systems simultaneously.

However, this framing underestimates what the workspace accomplishes. The workspace is not merely *integrating* information from different processors—it is *synthesizing new dimensions* through that integration.

**Principle 11.1 (Dimensional Synthesis).** The global workspace generates geometric structure that exists in none of the contributing spaces individually. Integration across spaces creates a higher-dimensional manifold onto which lower-dimensional spaces project.

| Goal projection | Possibility space (+) | Mapping beneficial future topology |

| Body state | Existence space | Current physical configuration |

**Definition 11.2 (Space-Specific Processor).** A *space-specific processor* P_s operates primarily within EGPT space S, with input/output signatures native to that space's structure.

**Definition 11.3 (Cross-Space Integration).** *Cross-space integration* occurs when the workspace combines outputs from processors in different spaces S₁, S₂, ..., Sₙ into a unified representation.

### 11.3 The Dimensional Synthesis Mechanism

**Theorem 11.4 (Synthesis Creates Dimension).** When the global workspace integrates information from spaces S₁ and S₂ with incompatible native structures, the integration manifold M has dimensionality:

dim(M) > max(dim(S₁), dim(S₂))

The additional dimension(s) encode the *relationship* between the spaces, which cannot be represented within either space alone.

**Example 11.5.** Integrating:
- Existence-space memory: "Last time I did X, outcome Y occurred" (collapsed past)
- Possibility-space projection: "If I do X now, topology suggests Z" (uncollapsed future)

Neither space can represent the *comparison* between past-Y and projected-Z. The workspace synthesizes a dimension where temporal comparison becomes geometrically representable.
