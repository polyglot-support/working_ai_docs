# Temporal Holonomy (TH) v0.1

*A holonomy framework for multi‑channel temporal loops and consistency in Agent‑Augmented Fractal Causality (AAFC).*

---

## 1. Tri‑temporal bundle and connection
- **Temporal base.** \(\mathbb T:=\mathbb R^3_{t_-,t_0,t_+}\). A **temporal loop** is a piecewise smooth closed curve \(\gamma:[0,1]\to\mathbb T\) with \(\gamma(0)=\gamma(1)\).
- **State bundle.** Pullback \(\Pi: \mathcal X:=\mathbb T\times X\to\mathbb T\), where \(X\) is the AAFC state bundle over spacetime (cf. AAFC v0.1 §0).
- **Channel generators.** Each channel induces a derivation/flow on payloads over \(X\): \(\mathcal L_- , \mathcal L_0 , \mathcal L_+\).
- **Temporal connection.**
  \[ \mathcal A \,=\, A_-\,\mathrm dt_-\; +\; A_0\,\mathrm dt_0\; +\; A_+\,\mathrm dt_+ \]
  with structure group \(G\) (e.g., \(\mathrm{U}(n)\) for quantum payloads, \(\mathrm{GL}(n)\) for classical). Gauge: \(\mathcal A\mapsto G^{-1}\mathcal A G + G^{-1}\mathrm d G\).

## 2. Temporal holonomy
For a closed temporal loop \(\gamma\) and a lift that begins/ends at the same fiber state, define
\[ H_\gamma\;:=\; \mathcal P\exp\Big(\oint_\gamma \mathcal A\Big)\in \mathrm{Hol}\subseteq G. \]
**Trivial TH** on \(\gamma\) means \(H_\gamma=\mathbb I\) in the chosen gauge. Nontrivial \(H_\gamma\) is a **temporal obstruction**.

## 3. Reduction checks
- **Single channel ⇒ Berry/AA phase.** If only \(t_0\) varies and \(A_-=A_+=0\), TH reduces to geometric phase holonomy for \(A_0\).
- **Standard SK contour ⇒ trivial TH.** On a forward–backward contour with matching Hamiltonians and unitarity/KMS, the composed transport is identity, so \(H_\gamma=\mathbb I\).

## 4. Compatibility theorem (small‑gain ⇒ trivial TH)
**Assumptions.** Transfers between charts/levels are generated by flows with inter‑channel gains \(K\) and dilations \(\Lambda\) (AAFC §5); atlas morphisms are \(\eta\)-contractive in a product metric; \(\mathcal A\) is built from these flows (compatibility).

**Claim.** If \(\rho(K\Lambda)<1\), then for every basis cycle \(\gamma\) in the temporal/transfer graph, \(H_\gamma=\mathbb I\). *Sketch:* Compose edge transports; contraction yields a unique fixed transport; loop product equals identity.

**Remark.** Without compatibility, small‑gain and TH are complementary tests; neither implies the other in general.

## 5. Counterexample template (temporal “impossible object”)
- **System.** Two‑level payload; \(A_0 = H(t)\) (Floquet), \(A_+ = J\) (coarse‑grain/jump), \(A_- = R\) (retro‑inference/adjoint). Each individually feasible under budgets.
- **Loop.** Segment 1 (\(t_0\)): evolve one period; Segment 2 (\(t_+\)): apply \(J\); Segment 3 (\(t_-\)): apply \(R\). Obtain \(H_\gamma\neq\mathbb I\) even when geometric reach is OK. Tune gains/metric to cross the small‑gain threshold and restore \(H_\gamma=\mathbb I\).

## 6. Cycle‑basis computation
1) Build graph of channel/level segments; nodes are chart/level states.
2) Attach edge transports \(T_e = \mathcal P\exp\int_e \mathcal A\).
3) Compute fundamental cycle basis \(\{\gamma_j\}\).
4) Evaluate holonomies \(H_{\gamma_j}=\prod_{e\in\gamma_j}T_e\); report defects and minimal repairs.

## 7. Experimental sketch (tri‑segment protocol)
- **Platforms:** NMR, superconducting qubits, trapped ions.
- **Steps:** forward evolve (\(H(t)\)) → controlled coarse‑grain \(J\) → retro‑inference \(R\). Measure a defect \(\|H_\gamma-\mathbb I\|\). Two‑segment tests may pass while tri‑segment reveals nontrivial TH.
